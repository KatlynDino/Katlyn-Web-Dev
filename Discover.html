FAMOUS TOURIST SPOT IN THE PHILIPPINES

MAYON

Mayon is an active stratovolcano in the Philippines, renowned for its near-perfect conical shape. Located in Albay province, it is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of natural beauty and geological activity.

PALAWAN

Palawan is a Philippine province known for its stunning natural landscapes and crystal-clear waters. Famous attractions include the Underground River, El Nido limestone cliffs, and Coron diving spots.

BORACAY

Boracay is a small island in the Philippines known for its white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. It's a popular tourist destination for relaxation, water sports, and beach parties.

CHOCOLATE HILLS

The Chocolate Hills in Bohol, Philippines, are a formation of over 1,260 conical hills spread across 50 square kilometers. Covered in green grass that turns brown in the dry season, they resemble chocolate mounds, giving them their name.

INTRAMUROS

Intramuros, located in Manila, Philippines, is a historic walled city built during the Spanish colonial period. Known for its preserved architecture, cobblestone streets, and significant landmarks, it serves as a cultural and historical hub.

BANAUE RICE TERRACES

The Banaue Rice Terraces are one of the most striking sights in the Philippines. The emerald-green terraces are an agricultural wonder in this area. Some of the terraces are estimated to have been hand-carved into the mountainside more than 2,000 years ago.

BAGUIO

BaguioIt is known as the "Summer Capital of the Philippines", owing to its cool climate since the city is located approximately 4,810 feet (1,470 meters) above mean sea level, often cited as 1,540 meters (5,050 feet) in the Luzon tropical pine forests ecoregion

VIGAN

Vigan Vigan, officially the City of Vigan (Ilocano: Siudad ti Vigan; Filipino: Lungsod ng Vigan), is a 4th class component city and capital of the province of Ilocos Sur, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 53,935 people.

TAGAYTAY

Samal island, also called the Garden City of Samal, is known for hiking, diving, and kayaking. One of the top things to do on Samal Island, besides relaxing at the beach, is visit the Monfort Bat Sanctuary, which is home to millions of bats in the caves.
